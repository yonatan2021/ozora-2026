# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Festival-goers at Ozora 2026 (international psytrance festival, Hungary, July 25 – Aug 3). Mixed crowd: experienced psychonauts, first-timers, international travelers. Using the site on phones outdoors — blazing sun during the day, pitch darkness at night, often in altered states. Need to find who's playing, when, on which stage — fast.

## Product Purpose
A timetable companion that feels like stepping into the festival itself. Not a tool that shows data — an experience that channels the Ozora energy: psychedelic, cosmic, rooted in nature, alive with electronic pulse. Success = people open it and feel the festival before they even arrive.

## Brand Personality
Psychedelic · Cosmic · Alive

The intersection of sacred geometry and electronic music, forest spirits and fractal energy. Ozora's DNA is hippie-mystical but forward-looking — nature worship meets futuristic sound design. The design should feel like the visual field at 3am on the main stage: deep, layered, breathing.

## Anti-references
- **Corporate SaaS apps**: no white-card grids, no clean utilitarian tool aesthetic, no safe neutral palettes. The current site IS this anti-reference.
- **Generic festival apps**: no Coachella/Tomorrowland cookie-cutter layouts with Instagram-ready minimalism. This is psytrance, not mainstream EDM.
- **Flat material design**: no Google-style flat surfaces. Depth, texture, and atmosphere are mandatory.
- **Overdesigned chaos**: bold ≠ unreadable. The timetable still needs to be scannable at a glance. Function serves the trip, not the other way around.

## Design Principles
1. **Atmosphere over interface** — every surface should carry mood. If it could be any app, it's wrong.
2. **Night-first, sun-proof** — the primary experience is after dark, but daytime readability in direct sunlight is non-negotiable. Both extremes must feel intentional, not adapted.
3. **Alive, not animated** — motion should feel organic and breathing, like the festival itself. No mechanical transitions, no corporate micro-interactions. Things pulse, flow, and shift.
4. **Depth through layers** — use blur, glow, transparency, and shadow to create spatial depth. The screen is a portal, not a flat surface.
5. **Stage identity is sacred** — each stage has a distinct color/energy. These aren't just labels — they're wayfinding in a disoriented state. Make them unmissable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- WCAG AA compliance minimum
- High contrast essential: readable in direct sunlight AND darkness (the site has time-of-day themes — each must pass independently)
- Stage colors must maintain distinctiveness for color-blind users
- Reduced motion alternative for all animations (already has `prefers-reduced-motion` support — maintain it)
- Large touch targets for outdoor/impaired-dexterity use
