## Public Showers & Water Availability

The festival grounds have fixed public shower facilities open to attendees 24 hours a day:

*   **Water Temperature:** Note that the water in these showers is very cold and unheated. There is no hot water supply at the public showers.
*   **Peak Hours & Lines:** Mornings (from 08:00 to 11:00) are the busiest, and you may face long lines during this window.
*   **Tip for Refreshing Up:** Showering during the hot afternoon hours (13:00–16:00) is an effective way to cool down and freshen up quickly and comfortably.

## Restrooms & Hygiene

Restroom facilities are spread throughout the festival and camping areas and are cleaned twice a day by the production's maintenance crew:

*   **Maintenance Level:** Despite regular cleaning, given the large number of attendees, cleanliness can decline by the end of the day.
*   **What to Bring:** Carry your own toilet paper and hand sanitizer whenever you head to the restrooms.
*   **Nighttime Use:** A headlamp is essential for safely and comfortably navigating the unlit public restrooms at night.

## Setting Up Your Own Camp Shower

A recommended alternative used by festival veterans:

*   **Gear Needed:** You can buy in advance (online or at camping stores) a solar camp shower — a dark 20-liter water bag you can hang from a tree branch — along with a pop-up shower tent.
*   **How It Works:** Set up the water bag and shower tent at your campsite. The sun heats the water throughout the day, letting you enjoy a private, warm shower right next to your tent — no walk to the public showers or waiting in line required.
