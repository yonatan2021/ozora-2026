## Ground Camping & Setting Up Your Tent

The most popular and recommended way to experience Ozora Festival is tent camping in one of the large camping areas spread across the grounds.

*   **Choosing a Tent Spot:** The northern camping area (near the forest) is especially recommended for the natural shade the trees provide, but it tends to fill up fast. We recommend arriving as early as possible to secure a good spot.
*   **Preparing the Ground:** Before pitching your tent, make sure the ground is level and clear of rocks, branches, or thorns that could damage your mattress or the tent itself.
*   **Quiet vs. Noise:** Attendees who want relatively quiet sleep should camp away from stages that run at night (such as Pumpui and the main stage). The western camping area is considered quieter and calmer.
*   **Marking Your Camp:** We recommend dropping a pin on Google Maps for your tent's location, making it easier to find at night or in the dark.

## Arriving & Staying in a Campervan / RV

Arriving at the festival with a campervan offers a lot of comfort, but requires preparing ahead of time:

*   **Utility Hookups:** Note that there are no electricity or running-water hookups for campervans within the festival grounds. Attendees must rely entirely on the vehicle's own batteries and built-in water tanks.
*   **Cutting Rental Costs:** Renting a campervan in Hungary itself is expensive, and availability is low during festival season. A recommended alternative is renting one in a neighboring country (such as Slovakia or Austria) and driving it to the festival.

## Parking a Car Next to Your Tent

Some attendees prefer to park their own car right next to their personal tent site:

*   **Securing Gear:** This makes it easy to securely store valuables and documents in a locked car while you're out at the stages.
*   **Extra Shade:** We recommend bringing large shade nets and stretching them between the car and the tent to create a comfortable, shaded hangout space.

## Community & Neighbors — Camp Life

Ozora Festival is built on values of community and mutual support:

*   Introduce yourself to your camp neighbors as soon as you set up.
*   Keep an eye on each other's gear and tents while your neighbors are out at the stages.
*   Be generous with each other — share water or food, and help keep the atmosphere warm and welcoming.
