## The Main Canteen

The most affordable and popular option for hot, filling meals during the festival:

*   **Menu Variety:** The canteen serves traditional Hungarian dishes, soups, meat stews, as well as a range of vegetarian and vegan options.
*   **Prices:** Canteen prices are regulated by the production team and are significantly lower than other food stalls.
*   **Service & Lines:** During peak meal hours (lunch and dinner) there can be long lines, but service is fast and efficient, and the line moves quickly.

## Food Stalls on Main Street

The festival's Main Street offers a wide range of food stalls open almost around the clock:

*   Fresh pizza baked on-site in wood-fired ovens.
*   Falafel and Mediterranean food stalls (a great option for a quick, filling meal).
*   Traditional Hungarian lángos — fried dough served with sour cream and cheese.
*   Asian-style stir-fries, fresh fruit smoothies, ice cream, and assorted desserts.
*   Gluten-free and vegan options are available at most stalls.

## Stocking Up Outside the Festival

To save money, we recommend combining on-site purchases with shopping outside the festival:

*   **The Festival Grocery Store:** A local grocery store operates within the festival grounds, offering basics like fresh bread, dairy, vegetables, fruit, and bottled water. Prices there are relatively high.
*   **Supermarkets in the Nearby Town:** A much cheaper option is taking the local shuttle to the nearby town of **Simontornya**, home to large, affordable **Lidl** and **Tesco** supermarkets. This is a great opportunity to stock up on water, snacks, self-catering supplies, and drinks at regular local prices.

## Cooking Your Own Food at the Campsite

Self-catering is allowed, subject to safety guidelines:

*   **Using Camp Stoves:** Small camping stoves (with disposable gas canisters) are allowed for cooking near your tent.
*   **Open Flames Strictly Prohibited:** Bonfires and open flames of any kind are strictly forbidden in the camping areas due to serious fire risk during the dry summer season.
*   **Pack Ahead of Time:** Bring energy bars, canned goods (tuna, legumes), black coffee, and tea bags to cut down on daily food costs.
