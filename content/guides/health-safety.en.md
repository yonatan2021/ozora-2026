## Dealing with Weather & Ground Conditions

Hungary's weather in July is extreme and can change quickly and suddenly:

*   **Daytime Heat:** Temperatures can rise above 35°C (95°F). Drink plenty of water (at least 3 liters a day), stay in shaded areas during the hottest afternoon hours, and use electrolyte supplements to prevent dehydration.
*   **Cold Nights:** Overnight and early-morning temperatures can drop to around 12°C (54°F). Pack warm clothing in advance — long layers, thick socks, and a proper jacket — to avoid getting cold at the stages or in your tent.
*   **Dealing with Dust:** When strong winds blow, the site can get extremely dusty. Bring a bandana or cloth mask to cover your nose and mouth.
*   **Sudden Storms:** Sudden summer thunderstorms occasionally hit the area, flooding the ground and creating mud. Make sure your tent is properly waterproofed and pitch it on relatively high ground to avoid flooding.

## Medical & First Aid Services

A professional medical team is available around the clock (24/7):

*   **Main Medical Center:** Located at the center of the festival grounds and marked with a large white flag bearing a red cross. Staffed with doctors, paramedics, and ambulances equipped to treat cuts, burns, injuries, stings, and dehydration.
*   **Camping First Aid Point:** Located in the southern camping area, for quick treatment of minor medical issues.
*   In any medical emergency, immediately approach one of the production staff or stewards stationed around the grounds.

## Emotional Support & Harm Reduction — The Haven Project

The **Haven** space offers mental health and emotional support to festival attendees:

*   **Emotional Support:** If you or your friends experience anxiety, sensory overload, confusion, or difficulty from a challenging psychedelic experience, a trained team of volunteers and experienced counselors is available to help.
*   **A Safe, Quiet Space:** The space offers a protected area to rest, drink warm tea, and receive attentive, non-judgmental professional support. Help is given privately, without judgment, and without involving stewards, security, or police.

## Protecting Your Belongings & Personal Security

Despite the festival's collaborative, community-driven atmosphere, it's wise to take extra care with your personal belongings:

*   **Securing Your Tent:** Lock your tent zippers with a small padlock when you're away. That said, a lock isn't foolproof — **never leave important documents or valuables in your tent** (such as a passport, cash, credit cards, or expensive electronics).
*   **Carrying Valuables:** Keep your travel documents and money in a close-fitting body bag (fanny pack) that stays with you at all times.
*   Keep extra-close watch on your car keys while you're out at the stages.
*   Build good relationships with your camping neighbors and look out for each other's tents and shared gear.
