## Buying Camping Gear in Hungary

Most Israeli travelers choose to buy their camping gear at **Decathlon** in Budapest:

*   **Order Pickup Window:** You can order in advance on Decathlon Hungary's website and pick it up at your chosen branch. Note that the package is held at the store for **only 7 days** from the order date.
*   **Availability & Stock:** Due to high demand and heavy traffic in stores during the summer and festival months, gear can sell out quickly and prices may rise. We recommend placing your order right at the start of the 7-day window before you arrive.
*   **Alternative Stores:** If certain items are out of stock at Decathlon, you can find basic, affordable camping gear at large supermarket chains like **Auchan**, or home-and-garden stores like **OBI** in Budapest.

## Recommended Camping Gear List

Packing the right gear is essential for a comfortable stay throughout the festival:

*   A quality tent with strong water and wind resistance (sudden summer storms are common in Hungary).
*   A good inflatable mattress or a thick sleeping pad (avoid thin yoga mats).
*   Quality earplugs and an eye mask (essential for restful sleep during daylight hours).
*   A headlamp (essential for getting around at night and in unlit restroom areas).
*   A clothesline and clothespins for hanging clothes and towels to dry.
*   A collapsible water container (to keep water accessible near your tent).

## Sun & Heat Protection

Daytime temperatures can get extremely high:

*   High-SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+) — bring enough to last the whole festival.
*   A wide-brimmed hat and sunglasses to protect your face and eyes.
*   A misting spray bottle — a great way to cool off while you're out at the stages.
*   A portable USB-rechargeable fan.

## First Aid & Personal Hygiene Kit

Taking care of your health and hygiene will help you avoid getting sick:

*   Salt and electrolyte supplements (powder or tablets) — essential for preventing dehydration from heat and physical exertion.
*   Basic pain relievers and medication (such as paracetamol or ibuprofen).
*   Band-aids, basic bandages, and antiseptic ointment for minor cuts.
*   Hand sanitizer to stay clean around the site.
*   Toilet paper and wet wipes (keep these in your personal bag at all times).
